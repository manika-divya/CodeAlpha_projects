\# 📊 CodeAlpha - Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)



\## 📌 Project Overview



This project was completed as part of the CodeAlpha Data Analytics Internship.



The objective of this project is to perform Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the Superstore Sales dataset to understand business performance, identify trends, detect patterns, and generate meaningful insights that can support business decision-making.



\---



\## 📂 Dataset



Dataset Used: Superstore Sales Dataset



The dataset contains information about:



\- Orders

\- Customers

\- Products

\- Categories

\- Sales

\- Profit

\- Discount

\- Region

\- Shipping Details



\---



\## 🎯 Objectives



\- Understand the structure of the dataset

\- Clean the data

\- Check missing values

\- Remove duplicate records

\- Perform statistical analysis

\- Explore business questions

\- Identify trends and patterns

\- Generate business insights



\---



\## 🛠️ Tools and Libraries Used



\- Python

\- Pandas

\- NumPy

\- Matplotlib

\- Seaborn

\- Jupyter Notebook



\---



\## 📈 Business Questions



The following questions were analyzed:



1\. Which region has the highest sales?

2\. Which category generates the highest profit?

3\. Which state has the highest sales?

4\. Which customer contributes the highest revenue?

5\. Which customer segment performs the best?

6\. What are the monthly sales trends?

7\. Which ship mode is most frequently used?

8\. Which products generate losses?



\---



\## 📊 Visualizations Created



\- Sales by Region

\- Profit by Category

\- Top 10 States by Sales

\- Monthly Sales Trend

\- Sales by Customer Segment

\- Correlation Heatmap

\- Profit Distribution

\- Sales vs Profit Scatter Plot



\---



\## 🔍 Key Insights



\- The highest sales were generated from the West region.

\- Technology was the most profitable category.

\- California recorded the highest sales.

\- Consumer was the largest customer segment.

\- A few products generated negative profit despite high sales.

\- Monthly sales showed noticeable seasonal variations.
