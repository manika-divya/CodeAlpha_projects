\# 📊 CodeAlpha - Data Visualization



\## 📌 Project Overview



This project was completed as part of the \*\*CodeAlpha Data Analytics Internship\*\*.



The objective of this project is to transform raw sales data into meaningful visualizations that help identify trends, patterns, and business opportunities. Different charts were created using Python libraries to support data-driven decision-making.



\---



\## 📂 Dataset



\*\*Dataset Used:\*\* Superstore Sales Dataset



The dataset contains information about:



\* Orders

\* Customers

\* Products

\* Categories

\* Sales

\* Profit

\* Discount

\* Shipping

\* Regions

\* States



\---



\## 🎯 Objectives



\* Visualize sales performance across different regions.

\* Analyze category-wise profit.

\* Identify top-performing states.

\* Understand monthly sales trends.

\* Analyze customer segments.

\* Study the relationship between sales and profit.

\* Present insights through clear and effective visualizations.



\---



\## 🛠️ Tools and Libraries Used



\* Python

\* Pandas

\* Matplotlib

\* Seaborn

\* Jupyter Notebook



\---



\## 📊 Visualizations Included



\* Sales by Region (Bar Chart)

\* Profit by Category (Bar Chart)

\* Top 10 States by Sales (Bar Chart)

\* Monthly Sales Trend (Line Chart)

\* Sales by Customer Segment (Pie Chart)

\* Correlation Heatmap

\* Sales vs Profit (Scatter Plot)

\* Profit Distribution (Histogram)



\---



\## 📈 Key Insights



\* The West region generated the highest sales.

\* Technology was the most profitable category.

\* California recorded the highest sales.

\* The Consumer segment contributed the largest share of sales.

\* Monthly sales showed noticeable seasonal variations.

\* Some orders generated high sales but resulted in negative profit.
